About

I am a cognitive scientist and passionate dancer, interested in how social interaction, embodiment, and neurodiversity shape human–human and human–machine interaction. My research combines experimental psychology with participatory methods to investigate interpersonal coordination.

Research

My work investigates social cognition in neurodiverse populations, focusing on how context shapes individual experience. I use immersive approaches such as virtual reality, dual eye-tracking, and participatory methods.

Comprehensive Autistic Traits Inventory (CATI-R)

The CATI-R is a revised and validated self-report questionnaire developed in partnership with autistic people to provide an inclusive, accessible, and reliable measure of autistic traits.
